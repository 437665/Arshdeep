BLUE SHARK

The blue shark is a particular kind of shark that inhabits warm, deep ocean waters. The shark is ten feet long, pale blue in colour, and has a slender frame. The blue shark is a marine creature that lives in oceans all over the world and is capable of travelling great distances. They eat squid and small fish, and they can give birth to up to 135 children at once. Because to overfishing, blue sharks are thought to be almost threatened despite their wide range. They don't pose a threat to humans, but if they are provoked, they might turn hostile. They must be protected because blue sharks are essential to the habitats in which they live.
